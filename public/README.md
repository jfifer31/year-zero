# Public Guide to Year Zero Education

Year Zero Education is an open-source project to design a new kind of curriculum for young people growing up in the Intelligence Era.

It asks:

> What should young people learn, experience, practise and demonstrate in order to become flourishing, capable and adaptive adults in a world increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence?

This public guide is for parents, learners, educators, mentors, contributors and anyone trying to understand the project without reading the full research system.

---

## 1. The short version

Year Zero Education is trying to build a first public version of a future-facing curriculum for KS3–KS4 learners.

The project does not begin with existing subjects and ask how to update them.

It begins with human life, flourishing and plausible AI-shaped futures, then works forward toward capabilities, learning experiences and evidence of learning.

The first output will be **Version 0.1**: a curriculum hypothesis that can be used, criticised, tested and improved.

---

## 2. Why this matters

Young people are growing up in a world where artificial intelligence may change:

- how people access knowledge;
- how work is done;
- how creativity is produced;
- how truth and trust are negotiated;
- how people make decisions;
- how people learn;
- how people form identity;
- what kinds of human capability remain valuable.

The answer cannot simply be “teach everyone to use AI tools”.

The deeper question is what kind of human beings young people need to become when tools become more intelligent.

---

## 3. What Version 0.1 will be

Version 0.1 will be:

- a first curriculum hypothesis;
- open-source;
- traceable to a research process;
- designed for KS3–KS4, with a KS3-first release;
- usable by parents, educators, mentors and motivated learners;
- honest about uncertainty;
- open to public feedback and improvement.

---

## 4. What Version 0.1 will not be

Version 0.1 will not be:

- a proven curriculum;
- a formal qualification;
- a replacement for school;
- a guarantee of learner outcomes;
- an official assessment system;
- a prediction of the future;
- a finished answer.

It is a serious starting point.

---

## 5. Who this is for

### Parents and home educators

The project may help you think about what your child needs beyond conventional content coverage: agency, judgement, capability, evidence of learning and meaningful projects.

### Educators

The project may help you compare current curriculum structures against a first-principles framework and experiment with future-facing enrichment, projects or evidence models.

### Learners

The project may help you understand your own learning as more than grades: what you can build, judge, explain, practise, improve and contribute.

### Researchers and experts

The project invites serious critique of its evidence, assumptions, future models, values, capability derivation and curriculum architecture.

### Builders and contributors

The project needs people who can improve documentation, design tools, test activities, give feedback and challenge weak thinking.

---

## 6. How the research works

The project follows a staged chain:

```text
Human Baseline
→ Human Flourishing
→ AI Future Conditions
→ Life Within Future Conditions
→ Human Invariants
→ Enduring Human Functions
→ Capabilities
→ Knowledge, Experience and Practice
→ Evidence of Learning
→ Curriculum Architecture
→ Verification and Release
```

This matters because the curriculum should not simply be a list of trendy skills. It should be derived from a clear chain of reasoning.

## 8. How to use early materials safely

Before Version 0.1 is fully released, treat any materials as exploratory.

Do use them cautiously, keep early testing low-stakes and protect learner privacy. Do not submit or publish private learner data, learner work, images, videos or AI logs.

Follow the full safeguarding, privacy, consent/assent, public-testing and promotional-use rules in `docs/ETHICS_AND_PUBLIC_TESTING.md`.

---

## 9. How to contribute

There are several useful ways to contribute:

- challenge a claim;
- suggest better evidence;
- report implementation feedback;
- identify access or safeguarding concerns;
- improve public explanation;
- test small learning activities;
- help make the project clearer.

Start with `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

---

## 10. The public promise

Year Zero Education should be ambitious without becoming overconfident.

It should be open enough to invite public participation and rigorous enough to deserve serious critique.

Its purpose is not to announce a finished answer. Its purpose is to build a better starting point for asking what education should become.
