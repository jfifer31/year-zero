# Content Strategy

This file defines how Year Zero Education should be explained publicly through writing, video, social media and release communications.

The goal is to make the project understandable, trustworthy and compelling without overclaiming.

**Status:** Stage 0 communication plan. Draft campaign language and content ideas are not research findings, curriculum decisions or release claims. They must be revalidated against the relevant stage outputs before publication.

---

## 1. Public communication mission

Public communication should help people understand:

- why education may need to be rethought for the Intelligence Era;
- why the project begins from first principles;
- how the research chain works;
- why Version 0.1 is a hypothesis;
- how parents, educators, learners and contributors can engage;
- what remains uncertain;
- why public critique is welcome.

Public communication must not request or publish private learner data, learner work, images, videos or AI logs. Follow `docs/ETHICS_AND_PUBLIC_TESTING.md` for safeguarding, privacy, public-testing and promotional-use rules.

---

## 2. Tone

The tone should be:

- clear;
- serious;
- warm;
- curious;
- accessible;
- intellectually honest;
- optimistic without hype;
- ambitious without certainty theatre.

Avoid:

- “schools are obsolete” rhetoric;
- doom-heavy AI messaging;
- techno-utopian claims;
- overconfident predictions;
- academic fog;
- marketing language that implies proven impact;
- making parents feel guilty or panicked.

---

## 3. Core public message

A simple public explanation:

> Year Zero Education is an open-source attempt to rethink curriculum from first principles for young people growing up in the Intelligence Era. Instead of starting with inherited subjects, it asks what human beings need to become when access to intelligence becomes abundant, then works back toward capabilities, learning experiences and evidence of learning.

---

## 4. Short description

> An open-source curriculum research project for young people growing up in the Intelligence Era.

---

## 5. Medium description

> Year Zero Education is building a Version 0.1 curriculum hypothesis for KS3–KS4 learners. It starts from human nature, flourishing and plausible AI-shaped futures, then derives the capabilities, knowledge, experiences and evidence of learning young people may need to become flourishing, capable and adaptive adults.

---

## 6. Long description

> Most curriculum debates begin with existing subjects and ask how they should be updated. Year Zero Education begins earlier. It asks what remains humanly important in a world increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence, what young people need to become, and what learning experiences could help them develop those capabilities. The first release, Version 0.1, will be an open-source curriculum hypothesis: traceable, usable, public, imperfect and designed for critique.

---

## 7. Key audience messages

### Parents

This project asks whether young people may need development beyond content coverage and exam preparation. Specific capabilities must be derived by the research chain before they are promoted publicly.

### Educators

This is a research-derived curriculum hypothesis you can challenge, adapt, compare and test. It is not a finished programme or an attack on schools.

### Learners

This project is investigating what learners may need to understand, practise and demonstrate. It has not yet decided the answer.

### Researchers

The project invites serious critique of its evidence, assumptions, values, future models and derivation chain.

### Builders and contributors

The project needs help turning research into usable curriculum structures, tools, feedback systems and public explanations.

## 8. Content pillars

### Pillar 1 — Why education needs a deeper question

Topics:

- Why adding AI tools is not enough.
- Why subjects are not the natural starting point.
- Why education should be derived from human life and future conditions.

### Pillar 2 — Human beings before technology

Topics:

- Motivation, belonging, agency and adolescence.
- What young people actually need to develop.
- Why flourishing is more than success.

### Pillar 3 — The Intelligence Era

Topics:

- What AI may change.
- What it may not change.
- Why future uncertainty matters.
- Why prediction is not the goal.

### Pillar 4 — What remains human

Topics:

- Human invariants.
- Judgement, care, meaning, embodiment, trust and responsibility.
- Why abundant intelligence does not remove the need for human capability.

### Pillar 5 — Capabilities, not slogans

Topics:

- How capabilities are derived.
- Why “21st-century skills” language is often too vague.
- How capability links to practice and evidence.

### Pillar 6 — Evidence of learning

Topics:

- Beyond exams versus anti-exam simplification.
- Portfolios, demonstrations, transfer and reflection.
- Why evidence must be meaningful and safe.

### Pillar 7 — Building in public

Topics:

- Why Version 0.1 is a hypothesis.
- How public critique improves the project.
- How parents, educators and learners can contribute.

## 10. Launch announcement structure

When Version 0.1 is ready, the announcement should include:

1. What has been released.
2. Why it exists.
3. Who it is for.
4. What it does not claim.
5. How it was derived.
6. How to use it.
7. How to challenge or contribute.
8. What happens next.

### Draft launch opening

> Today we are releasing Version 0.1 of Year Zero Education: an open-source curriculum hypothesis for young people growing up in the Intelligence Era. It is not a finished answer. It is a traceable starting point, built from a staged research process and designed to be challenged, tested and improved in public.

## 11. Social post themes

### Theme 1 — The core question

> What should young people learn when intelligence itself becomes abundant?

### Theme 2 — Version 0.1 humility

> Version 0.1 is not a claim to have solved education. It is a public, traceable starting point for building something better.

### Theme 3 — Human-first AI education

> The future of education is not just learning to use AI. It is learning what humans must still be able to judge, create, protect and become.

### Theme 4 — Evidence of learning

> Grades show some things. They do not show everything. Year Zero Education explores portfolios, demonstrations, artefacts, reflection and transfer as evidence of real capability.

### Theme 5 — Open contribution

> This project is open because the question is too important to answer privately. Parents, learners, educators, researchers and builders are invited to critique and improve it.

---

## 12. Claims to avoid

Do not say:

- “This curriculum is proven.”
- “This replaces school.”
- “Exams are useless.”
- “AI makes current education obsolete.”
- “This is the future of education.”
- “All learners need this exact framework.”
- “We know what the future will look like.”

Prefer:

- “This is a Version 0.1 hypothesis.”
- “This is designed for critique and testing.”
- “This explores what may need to change.”
- “This is traceable to a research process.”
- “This remains open to revision.”

---

## 13. Public trust rules

Public communication should always preserve:

- uncertainty;
- humility;
- evidence discipline;
- learner safety;
- respect for existing educators;
- openness to critique;
- clarity about experimental status.

Trust is part of the project infrastructure.

## 15. Final communication principle

The project should sound like a serious public laboratory for education, not a product launch.

It should make people feel invited into a difficult, important question — not pressured to buy a finished answer.
