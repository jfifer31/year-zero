# Year Zero Education – Version 0.1 Curriculum

This folder will contain the first public curriculum hypothesis produced by the Year Zero Education research chain.

**Version 0.1 is a curriculum hypothesis, not evidence of proven effectiveness.** It is not a final curriculum. It is the first coherent, traceable, open-source curriculum architecture generated from the project’s research process.

---

## Status

**Current status:** Not yet populated.  
**Required before completion:** Stages 1–11 must be completed, reviewed and passed forward.  
**Primary authorising stage:** Stage 10 – Curriculum Architecture and Version 0.1 Design.  
**Final release gate:** Stage 11 – Verification, Falsification and Release Readiness.

Do not fill this folder with substantive curriculum claims before Stage 10 has authorised them.

---

## Purpose of Version 0.1

Version 0.1 should provide a usable curriculum hypothesis for KS3–KS4 learners, with a KS3-first initial release.

It should help parents, educators, mentors and learners understand:

- what the curriculum is trying to develop;
- why those capabilities matter;
- what kinds of knowledge, experience and practice are included;
- how learning can be evidenced;
- how to begin using the framework;
- what remains uncertain or unproven;
- how to contribute feedback.

## Required release standard

Version 0.1 may be published only if it is:

- traceable to upstream research;
- honest about uncertainty;
- clear about its experimental status;
- practically usable;
- safe enough for exploratory use;
- structured for feedback;
- understandable to non-specialists;
- open to revision.

---

## What Version 0.1 must not claim

Version 0.1 must not claim that it:

- is proven effective;
- replaces school;
- replaces statutory curriculum obligations;
- provides formal qualifications;
- has solved assessment;
- works for every learner;
- predicts the future;
- is complete.

---

## Intended use

Version 0.1 may be used for:

- private exploration by families;
- educator review;
- small-scale low-stakes experimentation;
- curriculum critique;
- project development;
- public discussion;
- future pilot design.

It should not be used as high-stakes assessment, formal accreditation or evidence of learner superiority.

---

## Update process

Feedback from use should be routed into:

- implementation feedback issues;
- research challenge issues;
- Stage 11 release review;
- future Version 0.2 backlog.

Every major change after release should update `CHANGELOG.md` and, where relevant, `TRACEABILITY_MAP.md`.
