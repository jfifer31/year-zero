# Roadmap

This roadmap describes the execution sequence for Year Zero Education.

The project should move quickly, but not carelessly. It is designed to build a serious Version 0.1 curriculum hypothesis through a clear research chain, adversarial review and public release.

---

## Current phase

**Current phase:** Stage 0 complete; Stage 1 preparation

**Current objective:** Approve the Stage 1 execution plan without beginning research prematurely.

**Next substantive action:** Create and review the Stage 1 execution files, beginning with `STAGE_EXECUTION.md`.

## Phase 1 — Human Stream

### Stage 1 – Human Baseline

Determine the most defensible assumptions about human beings that an educational framework must respect.

Key areas include:

- motivation;
- agency;
- incentives;
- status;
- belonging;
- cooperation;
- competition;
- cognition;
- attention;
- learning;
- adolescence;
- embodiment;
- identity formation;
- moral development;
- emotional regulation;
- individual variation;
- social behaviour.

Output: a minimum defensible human baseline.

### Stage 2 – Human Flourishing Model

Use Stage 1 to define what flourishing should mean for the purposes of this project.

Output: a project-specific model of flourishing for young people in the Intelligence Era.

---

## Phase 2 — AI / Future Stream

### Stage 3 – AI Future Conditions Discovery

Discover plausible AI-shaped future conditions without forcing premature scenarios.

Output: a structured map of drivers, uncertainties, possible conditions and signposts.

---

## Phase 3 — Convergence

### Stage 4 – Life Within Future Conditions

Bring the human and AI streams together by modelling lived human life inside plausible future conditions.

Output: future-life models for learners, families, workers, communities and institutions.

### Stage 5 – Human Invariants Analysis

Identify what remains fundamentally human, valuable or unavoidable across plausible futures.

Output: human invariants that later stages must respect.

---

## Phase 4 — Derivation

### Stage 6 – Enduring Human Functions

Derive what humans must still do, decide, judge, create, coordinate, protect, experience or become.

Output: enduring human functions.

### Stage 7 – Capability Derivation

Translate enduring functions into developable capabilities.

Output: capability model.

### Stage 8 – Knowledge, Experience and Practice Mapping

Identify what knowledge, experiences and practices can cultivate those capabilities.

Output: learning architecture inputs.

---

## Phase 5 — Evidence and Curriculum Construction

### Stage 9 – Evidence of Learning and Verification Architecture

Define how learning and capability can be observed, demonstrated, reflected upon and eventually verified.

Output: evidence-of-learning architecture.

### Stage 10 – Curriculum Architecture and Version 0.1 Design

Build the Version 0.1 curriculum architecture from the previous stages.

Output: draft curriculum framework.

---

## Phase 6 — Release Readiness

### Stage 11 – Verification, Falsification and Release Readiness

Stress-test the full chain before release.

Output: release readiness decision, known limitations and public Version 0.1 package.

---

## Version 0.1 release package

The first public release should include:

- curriculum framework;
- implementation guide;
- evidence-of-learning model;
- limitations and open questions;
- traceability map;
- public FAQ;
- contribution pathway.

---

## Version 0.2 and beyond

Later versions should be driven by:

- research corrections;
- adversarial critique;
- learner feedback;
- parent and educator feedback;
- implementation results;
- changes in AI conditions;
- better evidence;
- public contribution.

Version 0.1 should therefore be designed for revision, not defended as final.

---

## Operating cadence

Each stage follows the operating cadence defined in `docs/RESEARCH_OPERATING_SYSTEM.md`: authorised inputs, execution plan, evidence review, stage ledger, report, adversarial review, pass-forward memo, public summary and gate decision.

Agent sequencing and role activation for that cadence are defined in `AGENTS.md`.
