## 2. Objective

Construct a provisional, source-grounded account of recurring human drives, psychological mechanisms and social structures without beginning from education, adolescence, flourishing or future scenarios.

## 4. Research sequence

1. Lock authority and stage boundaries.
2. Run eight workstreams independently.
3. Screen and deduplicate 160–220 sources.
4. Record source scope, limitations, contrary evidence and prohibited overextension.
5. Produce workstream evidence syntheses and candidate claims.
6. Conduct eight specialist talks.
7. Run a chaired cross-disciplinary challenge round.
8. Integrate a narrative report without educational implications.
9. Run evidence, bias, cultural-generalisability and adversarial audits.
10. Revise until audits pass.
11. Issue a component-only pass-forward to Stage 1R.

## 6. Acceptance rules

A source is accepted only if it directly informs a workstream question, has identifiable provenance, can support a bounded claim, and has its population, method and limitations recorded. The ledger must not treat theory, commentary or illustrative examples as anchor evidence.

Universality requires cross-cultural support. Innateness requires convergent developmental, cross-cultural and comparative support. Contemporary recurrence alone supports neither.

## 7. Specialist discussion protocol

Each specialist gives one or two source-backed talks answering:

> What recurrent feature of human action or social organisation do purpose-oriented accounts most often underestimate?

Each talk must state its claim, mechanism, source IDs, cross-cultural scope, strongest counterexample, implications for another discipline and prohibited overextension. Responses are permitted only to add a mechanism, challenge, cultural modifier, power/status implication or evidential boundary.

## 8. Review protocol

The Evidence Auditor checks support and causal language. The Bias and Generalisability Auditor checks WEIRD, class, gender, ecological and institutional assumptions. The Adversarial Red Team attacks evolutionary just-so stories, inevitability claims, hidden normative judgements and narrative inflation. The Claim Steward controls the component pass-forward.
