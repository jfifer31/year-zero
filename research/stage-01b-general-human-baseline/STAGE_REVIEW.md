## Decision

`pass_with_cautions`

The first audit returned `revise_before_pass`. The evidence corpus was accepted, but the integrated narrative overstated a provisional problem-domain framework, used causal and functional wording, incompletely specified claim records and mixed empirical findings with methodological and normative rules. The report and ledger were revised. Three independent targeted re-audits then returned `PASS`.

## Evidence audit

- 185 accepted, deduplicated sources are logged across eight workstreams.
- All cited source, evidence, claim, uncertainty and risk IDs exist.
- `S1B-EVD-001`–`S1B-EVD-008` trace every included source in their workstreams.
- Major claims state scope, limitations, counterevidence, confidence and prohibited overextension.
- Noncanonical confidence labels were removed.
- Measurement cautions were separated from ecological and institutional associations.
- Punishment outcomes were separated from judgements about justice.

## Bias and cultural-generalisability audit

The corpus remains English-language and Western-heavy, especially in agency, meaning and cognition. Balanced workstream counts do not imply balanced linguistic, regional, class, gender or institutional coverage. Country count is not cultural coverage, translation is not construct equivalence, and contemporary small-scale societies are not ancestral fossils.

Rank evidence underrepresents women, gender-diverse people, domestic power and less publicly visible forms of authority. Meaning taxonomies remain predominantly Western. Cooperation evidence is weighted toward games and selected field cases. These limitations are carried locally and into the component handoff.

## Adversarial audit

The review rejected:

- a species-wide problem–solution architecture as an empirical finding;
- recurrence as proof of innateness, adaptation, inevitability or legitimacy;
- hierarchy as one universal drive or status ladder;
- cooperation as evidence of justice;
- punishment as inherently prosocial;
- ritual, religion, contribution or sacrifice as necessarily beneficial;
- culture or biology as a total causal explanation.

The six-domain structure survives only as a revisable Working Assumption for Stage 1R. A power audit survives only as an explicit Normative Judgement and review protocol.

## Strongest retained findings

1. Regulation can be anticipatory and state-sensitive, while biomarkers do not transparently reveal experience.
2. Defence, conscious fear, wanting, liking and reward learning are partly distinguishable within reviewed paradigms.
3. Control beliefs, executable means, opportunities, habits and plans are distinguishable; they are not one agency trait.
4. Bounded attention and information are well supported; the value of particular heuristics or copying strategies remains context-dependent.
5. Reviewed social networks often include overlapping kin and non-kin relations; categorisation does not inherently produce hostility.
6. Prestige, dominance, authority, inheritance and resource control are distinguishable but non-exhaustive routes to influence.
7. Rank can become self-reinforcing under some conditions and can be constrained by resistance and levelling practices.
8. Cooperation and punishment outcomes vary with expectations, boundaries, rules, power and retaliation.
9. Western meaning research distinguishes several judgements, while ritual, protected values and contribution have conditional associations with commitment.
10. Measurement affects which differences are visible and comparable; ecology and institutions remain associational rather than settled causes.

## Uncertainties retained

`S1B-UNC-001`–`S1B-UNC-008` remain open, including cultural transportability, preparedness versus convergence, construct comparability, agency and automaticity, affiliation direction, status conversion, cooperation versus extraction, and meaning versus coercion. The report additionally carries gendered and less-visible systems as an explicit reconciliation question.
