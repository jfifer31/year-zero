# Stage 1B Public Summary — Recurring Problems, Variable Human Worlds

## What Stage 1B asked

Stage 1B examined recurring human drives, psychological processes and social structures outside education. It tested recurrence rather than assuming that familiar patterns are universal, innate, inevitable or beneficial.

## What it found

The evidence supports several bounded distinctions rather than one grand theory of human nature:

- bodily regulation can anticipate demand, but biomarkers do not transparently reveal experience;
- defence, fear, wanting, liking and reward learning are not interchangeable;
- control beliefs, available means, habits, plans and opportunities are distinguishable parts of action;
- attention and information are limited, while heuristics and social learning can preserve either useful knowledge or error;
- reviewed social networks often included overlapping kin and non-kin relationships, and group categories did not inherently produce hostility;
- prestige, dominance, authority, inheritance and resource control are different routes to influence;
- status can reinforce advantage and can also be resisted;
- cooperation and punishment changed with expectations, rules, boundaries, power and retaliation;
- meaning, ritual, protected values and contribution can organise commitment, but they can also accompany coercion, exclusion or conflict;
- measurement affects which cultural differences become visible and comparable.

## The central caution

Recurrence is not proof of innateness. A pattern may reflect biological preparedness, common developmental demands, convergent learning, diffusion, ecology, institutions or measurement. Existing hierarchy is not evidence that hierarchy is necessary or deserved. Cooperation is not automatically justice, and contribution is not automatically voluntary.

## Evidence limits

The review accepted 185 deduplicated sources across eight workstreams. It remains English-language and Western-heavy. Workstream balance is not cultural, linguistic, class or gender balance. Women, gender-diverse people, domestic power and less visible forms of authority remain underrepresented in several evidence areas. Laboratory games and Western-developed measures limit transportability.

## What happens next

Stage 1B passes its component gate with cautions. Its findings go only to Stage 1R, where they will be compared with the education-facing Stage 1A. Stage 2 remains blocked until that reconciliation passes.
