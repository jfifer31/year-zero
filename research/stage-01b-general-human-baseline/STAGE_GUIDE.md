# Stage 1B – General Human Baseline

## 1. Stage purpose

Stage 1B investigates recurring human drives, psychological mechanisms and social structures outside educational settings. It tests recurrence rather than assuming innateness and distinguishes evolved, developmental, cultural, ecological and institutional explanations.

## 2. Primary question

> What recurring drives, psychological mechanisms and social structures shape human action across contexts, and which are sufficiently defensible to constrain later reasoning about flourishing and human purpose?

## 3. Position and authority

**Depends on:** Stage 0 operating files only.

**Feeds into:** Stage 1R Human Baseline Reconciliation only.

Stage 1B cannot independently authorise Stage 2. Its component pass-forward is an input to reconciliation.

## 4. Workstreams

1. **W1 — Embodied drives and regulation:** homeostasis, threat, reward, attachment, caregiving, affect, stress and bodily regulation.
2. **W2 — Agency, control, exploration and mastery:** perceived control, competence, curiosity, play, exploration, habit and goal pursuit.
3. **W3 — Status, hierarchy and reputation:** dominance, prestige, rank, signalling, shame, pride, deference, inequality and mobility.
4. **W4 — Belonging, identity and coalitions:** kinship, affiliation, conformity, group identity, coalition formation, boundaries and exclusion.
5. **W5 — Cooperation, competition and institutions:** reciprocity, trust, free-riding, punishment, norm enforcement, collective action and institutional coordination.
6. **W6 — Meaning, morality and responsibility:** narrative, purpose, ritual, fairness, duty, sacred values, moral pluralism and contribution.
7. **W7 — Cognition and decision under uncertainty:** attention, bounded rationality, heuristics, imitation, social learning, prediction and motivated reasoning.
8. **W8 — Culture, variation and developmental emergence:** cross-cultural variation, ecological context, individual differences and emergence outside adolescence.

## 5. Evidence posture

- Use anthropology, sociology, social psychology, behavioural economics, cultural evolution, comparative behaviour, cognitive science and relevant biology.
- Prioritise reviews, meta-analyses, major cross-cultural datasets and strong replicated primary studies.
- Include at least one cross-cultural or anthropological anchor and one contrary or boundary-setting source per workstream.
- Do not infer evolutionary origin from contemporary prevalence.
- Use *innate* only when developmental, cross-cultural and comparative evidence jointly supports it.
- Distinguish recurrent tendencies from inevitable institutions or fixed outcomes.
- Re-screen any Stage 1A source and assign a new Stage 1B record and scope justification before reuse.

## 6. In scope

- recurrent human motives and drives;
- status, hierarchy, reputation and power;
- belonging, coalitions and group boundaries;
- cooperation, competition, reciprocity and norm enforcement;
- meaning, morality, ritual and responsibility;
- bounded cognition, social learning and decision-making;
- cross-cultural, ecological and individual variation;
- claims of recurrence, developmental emergence and innateness;
- tensions among mechanisms and institutions.

## 7. Out of scope

- educational implications or recommendations;
- KS3–KS4 or adolescent-development conclusions;
- capabilities, subjects, curriculum purposes or pedagogy;
- a final model of flourishing;
- AI futures or technological prediction;
- a total theory of human nature.

## 8. Method

Use a deep rapid evidence review of approximately 160–220 accepted sources, eight independent specialist syntheses, focused cross-disciplinary discussion, claim classification, and evidence, bias, cultural-generalisability and adversarial audits.

The study must preserve contrary evidence, causal limits, cultural variation, minority interpretations and uncertainty. Source counts are coverage controls, not votes.

## 9. Required outputs

The six canonical files must contain:

- Minimum General Human Baseline;
- Human Drives and Motives Map;
- Recurring Social Structures Map;
- Status, Power and Coalition Analysis;
- Cooperation–Competition–Norm Enforcement Tension Map;
- Culture and Variation Boundaries;
- Claims of Recurrence versus Claims of Innateness;
- Open Questions and Prohibited Overextensions;
- Component Pass-Forward for Stage 1R.

## 11. Gate conditions

Stage 1B may pass only when:

- 160–220 accepted sources are logged with balanced coverage;
- every workstream contains a cross-cultural or anthropological anchor and a contrary or boundary-setting source;
- major claims include class, confidence, scope, limitations, counterevidence and prohibited overextension;
- no correlational result is presented causally;
- no recurrent structure is described as biologically inevitable;
- no evolutionary explanation rests on plausibility alone;
- no WEIRD or institution-specific pattern is universalised;
- evidence, bias, cultural-generalisability and adversarial audits pass;
- its pass-forward explicitly limits authority to Stage 1R.
