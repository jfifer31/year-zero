## Working assumptions

### Six-domain reconciliation frame

Stage 1R may provisionally compare evidence through regulation, directed action, affiliation and boundaries, rank, collective action, and meaning or commitment. This is analytical, non-exhaustive and revisable, not an empirical architecture. [Ledger: `S1B-CLM-001`; uncertainties: `S1B-UNC-001`–`S1B-UNC-003`]

### Recurrence inference rule

Recurrence does not by itself establish universality, innateness, adaptation, inevitability, legitimacy or value. Alternative explanations include preparedness, common developmental demands, convergent learning, diffusion, institutional reproduction and measurement artefact. [Ledger: `S1B-CLM-013`]

## Normative reconciliation protocols

- Coordination or contribution is not automatically justice. [Ledger: `S1B-CLM-015`]
- Stage 1R should explicitly examine power, beneficiaries, casualties and credible resistance when reviewing hierarchy, cooperation, duty and meaning. The value basis is preventing naturalisation and hidden extraction; the trade-off is possible underweighting of coordination benefits or non-power explanations. [Ledger: `S1B-CLM-014`]

These are explicit project commitments, not empirical properties of humans.

## Open uncertainties

Stage 1R must carry `S1B-UNC-001`–`S1B-UNC-008` without resolution by rhetorical synthesis. It must also examine how gendered, domestic and less publicly visible systems affect apparent care, rank, authority and contribution.

## Prohibited overextensions

Do not infer:

- a fixed or exhaustive drive inventory;
- a species-wide problem–solution architecture;
- innate hierarchy, inevitable tribalism or natural inequality;
- one agency, autonomy, kinship, cooperation, meaning or moral system;
- evolutionary origin from recurrence or current usefulness;
- justice from cooperation, sanctions, ritual or contribution;
- causal cultural effects from ecological association;
- individual motive or subjective state from biomarkers, tasks or group averages.
