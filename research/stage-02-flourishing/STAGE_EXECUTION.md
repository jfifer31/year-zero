# Stage 2 — Human Flourishing Model: Execution Record

**Date:** 22 June 2026
**Owner:** Master Coordinator
**Status:** `closed — pass_with_cautions`

## Objective

Define a project-specific, plural and non-coercive model of flourishing by combining the authorised Stage 1R human baseline with a bounded rapid review of flourishing evidence and explicit normative reasoning. This stage defines what the project may value; it does not derive curriculum, subjects, capabilities, assessment or policy.

## Authorised inputs

| Input | Permitted use | Restriction |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1R `PASS_FORWARD.md` | Sole human-baseline interface | Constraints and questions, not a definition of the good life |
| Stage 2 scholarly and official sources | Evidence about well-being, needs, relationships, meaning, contribution, conditions and variation | Associations are not causal; constructs are culturally bounded |
| Explicit normative reasoning | Select and justify project values | Must be labelled `Normative Judgement`, with trade-offs and dissent |

## Boundaries

In scope: multidimensional well-being; agency; competence; relationships; meaning; contribution; responsibility; material and bodily conditions; pluralism; adolescent modifiers; value conflicts; measurement cautions.

Out of scope: treatment models, a comprehensive political doctrine, curriculum purposes, subjects, capabilities, pedagogy, assessment, AI futures or implementation design.

## Questions

1. Which dimensions recur across credible accounts and evidence of flourishing?
2. Which are empirical associations, enabling conditions or normative commitments?
3. How should agency, belonging, competence, meaning, contribution, responsibility and felt well-being be balanced?
4. How should the model protect plural lives, adolescent development and learner variation?
5. What must never be inferred from a flourishing score?

## Method

| Method | Purpose | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Normative–empirical synthesis | Keep evidence and values in one traceable argument | Evidence cannot settle what ought to matter |
| Rapid evidence review | Test dimensions against reviews, frameworks and major studies | Not a systematic review; English-language and accessible-source bias |
| Dimensions and trade-off mapping | Reveal complementarity and collision | Categories overlap and are not natural modules |
| Pluralism and power audit | Test culture, coercion, status and hidden burdens | Cannot represent every tradition or lived experience |
| Adversarial review | Attack ideology, measurability and misuse | Review cannot remove all downstream implementation risk |

## Agent harnesses

- Master Coordinator: dependency, integration and gate decision.
- Flourishing and Values Expert: dimensions and explicit value choices.
- Human Sciences and Adolescent Development Experts: empirical compatibility and developmental modifiers.
- Evidence Synthesis Researcher and Claim Steward: source weighting and classification.
- Bias/Generalisability and Adversarial reviewers: pluralism, coercion, measurement and overreach.
- Documentation Editor: one coherent report and controlled handoff.

## Evidence posture

Priority was given to systematic/scoping reviews, meta-analyses, major international frameworks and field-defining theoretical work. Sources were included only when they clarified a dimension, mechanism, boundary, adolescent modifier or measurement problem. Clinical-only findings were not generalised. Philosophical works inform normative alternatives, not empirical claims. Stage 1A sources were not silently imported; Stage 1R alone supplies the baseline.
