# Stage 2 — Human Flourishing Model

> **Status:** `pass_with_cautions`
> **Claim discipline:** empirical findings, working assumptions and normative judgements are labelled separately.
> **Boundary:** this report defines a project value model. It does not derive curriculum, capabilities, subjects, pedagogy or assessment.

## Executive Summary

Flourishing cannot be reduced to feeling good, doing well, fitting in or being useful. Each captures something important; each can also conceal a damaged life. Satisfaction can adapt to deprivation. Achievement can coexist with fear or emptiness. Belonging can require conformity. Purpose can attach to harmful ends. Contribution can be demanded from those least free to refuse.

The evidence instead supports a multidimensional and condition-sensitive picture. Experiences of volition, effectiveness and supportive connection recur as correlates of motivation and well-being across varied settings, but neither expression nor measurement is culturally uniform. Relationship quality matters beyond contact or popularity. A prominent Western meaning taxonomy distinguishes coherence, purpose and significance, while empirical associations remain largely adult and measure-dependent. [Evidence syntheses: `S2-EVD-001`–`S2-EVD-004`; claims: `S2-CLM-001`–`S2-CLM-005`, `S2-CLM-007`, `S2-CLM-008`]

Separately, Stage 2 adopts a bounded structural Working Assumption: material resources, health, safety, rights and institutional power condition which lives are genuinely available. Conceptual, official and cross-national frameworks inform this premise, but the reviewed set does not establish one causal structural account. [`S2-CLM-006`; evidence synthesis: `S2-EVD-005`]

Evidence alone cannot decide the project’s values. Stage 2 therefore adopts an explicit normative thesis:

> **Flourishing is the developing and revisable capacity to live a life one has reason to value: with sufficient bodily and material security, credible agency, growing competence, relationships that permit recognition without domination, sources of meaning that can be questioned, and opportunities for responsible contribution that do not depend on coerced sacrifice.**

This is a floor-and-freedom model, not a single picture of success. The floor protects bodily integrity, safety, rights, recognition and real opportunity. The freedom protects plural identities, relationships, purposes and ways of living. Responsibility appears inside flourishing, but not as obedience: it concerns learning to recognise other people as equally real, to understand consequences, to keep commitments one can legitimately hold, to repair harm, and to participate without surrendering the right to dissent or exit.

**Claim status:** the recurrence and association of several dimensions are Empirical Findings, generally moderate confidence. Their selection, balance and integration into the floor-and-freedom model are Normative Judgements. The model carries no empirical confidence label and must not become a learner score.

## 1. The Seduction of a Single Number

Every simple account of flourishing has an attraction. Happiness respects first-person experience. Preference satisfaction resists paternalism. Achievement recognises effort and capability. Belonging recognises that lives are relational. Meaning sees beyond comfort. Contribution refuses a purely private good life.

The problem begins when one becomes the master measure.

Flourishing scales are consistently multidimensional, but they disagree about dimensions and weights; hedonic and eudaimonic measures also overlap substantially. Life satisfaction is valuable evidence about a person’s appraisal, not a complete audit of freedom, safety, health or relationship quality. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-012–S2-A-SRC-015] Social connection illustrates the same problem. Network size, contact, perceived support, loneliness, relationship quality and safety are different constructs. Being surrounded, enrolled, popular or online does not establish recognition or care. [Sources: S2-B-SRC-001, S2-B-SRC-002, S2-B-SRC-004, S2-B-SRC-005, S2-B-SRC-010]

The first principle is therefore negative but powerful: **no single observable outcome is flourishing**. This follows Stage 1R’s warning that one performance, category, signal or reported state cannot transparently reveal a whole person. It also prevents a well-being vocabulary from becoming a gentler form of surveillance.

**Empirical Finding S2-CLM-001:** dimensions of feeling, functioning, relationship and condition provide partly nonredundant information, although their measures overlap. **Confidence:** Moderate. **Limit:** there is no evidence-derived universal weighting. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-012–S2-A-SRC-016]

## 2. From Choice to Credible Agency

Agency is often mistaken for the presence of choice. Yet a menu can be trivial, manipulated or impossible to use. Conversely, obligation can sometimes be endorsed rather than merely imposed. The more defensible empirical construct is volition: experiencing one’s action as meaningfully authored or endorsed, within actual relationships and constraints.

Reviews and multinational studies repeatedly associate autonomy or volition, competence and relatedness with adaptive motivation and well-being. Educational meta-analysis finds autonomous motivation generally associated with more adaptive outcomes, while controlled motivation is mixed and amotivation consistently adverse. But most evidence is observational, self-reported and theory-led; it supports relevance, not an exhaustive ontology of needs. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-001–S2-A-SRC-004, S2-A-SRC-007, S2-A-SRC-009]

Stage 1R adds a crucial distinction. Control beliefs, actual means, opportunity, habits, planning and motivational quality are not one trait. Confidence without means is not agency. Offered choice without viable options is not agency. Persistence can be compelled or maladaptive. Compliance cannot disclose endorsement.

The project therefore defines credible agency as the developing conjunction of:

- an intelligible range of options;
- sufficient knowledge and capability to act;
- material and social permission to use those options;
- a voice in decisions that materially affect one’s life;
- the ability to reconsider, refuse and seek help without disproportionate penalty.

The conjunction is a **Normative Judgement**, not a finding that all cultures express agency identically. It deliberately separates autonomy from atomistic independence. A person may freely value family duty, faith, interdependence or tradition. The test is not whether the life resembles an individualist ideal; it is whether endorsement is credible under the relevant relations of dependence and power.

**Empirical Finding S2-CLM-002:** volition, competence and relatedness are recurrent correlates of adaptive motivation and well-being across multiple populations. **Confidence:** Moderate. **Prohibited overextension:** three constructs do not exhaust flourishing, and choice does not prove autonomy. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-001–S2-A-SRC-004, S2-A-SRC-007, S2-A-SRC-009]

## 3. Competence Without the Achievement Trap

Humans often flourish through becoming able: understanding something difficult, making something work, keeping a promise, navigating a relationship, caring for another person, or recovering the capacity to act. Competence matters because it changes a person’s effective reach.

But competence is easily colonised by status. Confidence is not competence. A grade is not a complete measure of either. Public rank may reward prior opportunity, narrow performance or strategic conformity. Evidence on self-efficacy suggests that confidence and performance influence one another, with prior performance often predicting later confidence more strongly than the reverse. **Empirical Finding `S2-CLM-004`, Moderate confidence:** competence, confidence and performance are distinguishable and reciprocally related; no one measure establishes capability or worth. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-003, S2-A-SRC-005, S2-A-SRC-006; `S2-EVD-002`]

Stage 2 therefore values competence as **expanding effective and responsible action**, not maximising productivity, credentials or comparative rank. Competence includes knowing one’s limits, seeking collaboration and recognising when a system—not the person—creates the barrier. It is compatible with disability and dependency; human capability is often distributed across tools, relationships and institutions.

Challenge also needs careful treatment. A good life is not frictionless, and growth can involve frustration, uncertainty and failure. Yet this does not license preventable adversity. “Resilience” names adaptation under difficulty; it must not become praise for systems that keep producing avoidable harm.

**Normative Judgement S2-NRM-002:** competence is valuable as effective engagement and enlarged possibility, while achievement, productivity and status remain subordinate and contestable proxies.

## 4. Recognition, Care and the Right Not to Belong

Supportive connection is among the most consistent correlates of well-being and lower distress. Meta-analyses and longitudinal work associate perceived support, friendship quality, warm family relations and school connectedness with multiple positive outcomes. Relationship quality and safety matter beyond quantity. [Sources: S2-B-SRC-003–S2-B-SRC-011, S2-B-SRC-020]

Yet “belonging” can be dangerously sentimental. Groups can protect insiders by humiliating outsiders. Families can care and control. Schools can produce high belonging scores by rewarding conformity. Peer groups can transmit harmful norms, and closeness can intensify co-rumination. Selection and influence are entangled: similar people choose one another and become more similar through interaction. [Sources: S2-B-SRC-005, S2-A-SRC-011, S2-B-SRC-018, S2-B-SRC-019; duplicate S2-B-SRC-007 is non-decision]

The relational good Stage 2 values is therefore not compulsory affiliation. It is **recognition without domination**: access to relationships in which a person can receive and offer age-appropriate care, remain intelligibly themselves, exercise privacy, disagree, and leave harmful ties where exit is reasonably possible.

This formulation protects chosen solitude and low-social-intensity lives. It rejects popularity and continual participation as proxies. **Empirical Finding `S2-CLM-007`, Moderate confidence:** family, peer, teacher and school relationships are distinguishable sources of connection associated with adolescent outcomes. The Western-heavy, school-enrolled evidence does not establish strict non-substitutability or one formula. [Sources: S2-B-SRC-004, S2-A-SRC-010, S2-B-SRC-016, S2-B-SRC-020; `S2-EVD-003`]

**Empirical Finding S2-CLM-003:** supportive and safe relationship quality is associated with well-being and lower distress, but causal pathways are reciprocal and context-sensitive. **Confidence:** Moderate. **Prohibited overextension:** connection does not diagnose or treat mental illness, and cohesion does not establish justice.

## 5. Meaning That Can Survive Questioning

Pleasure answers “How does life feel?” Meaning asks “How does this life hang together, where is it going, and why does it matter?” A prominent Western conceptual review distinguishes coherence, purpose and significance or mattering; this is a useful taxonomy, not a universal one. **Empirical Finding `S2-CLM-005`, Moderate confidence:** meaning and purpose show bounded associations with well-being and health, though evidence is mostly adult, correlational and measure-dependent. [Sources: S2-SRC-044, S2-A-SRC-022; `S2-EVD-004`]

Meaning is not self-validating. Harmful causes can provide coherence. Sacred values can intensify conflict. A role can feel meaningful because refusal is costly or unimaginable. Purpose can become an instrument through which institutions demand sacrifice while calling it fulfilment.

The model therefore values **meaning with reflexivity**: access to narratives, commitments and purposes that make action intelligible, combined with permission to question their truth, moral worth and ownership. A flourishing life need not contain one grand mission. Local attachments, craft, care, curiosity, faith, beauty and ordinary responsibility may each organise significance.

Contribution receives the same discipline. **Empirical Finding `S2-CLM-008`, Moderate confidence:** meta-analytic evidence links prosocial behaviour modestly and heterogeneously with well-being, and perceived positive impact may add value. These associations do not prove that imposed service, unpaid care or self-erasure are flourishing. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-019, S2-A-SRC-021; `S2-EVD-004`]

**Normative Judgement S2-NRM-004:** contribution is valuable when it respects the standing of recipients, distributes burdens fairly, remains developmentally appropriate and preserves credible voice or refusal. Social usefulness is not the price of human worth.

## 6. The Floor Beneath Freedom

Psychological language can make flourishing sound like a quality generated inside the person. Stage 2 adopts the bounded Working Assumption that health, bodily integrity, safety, material resources, rights, discrimination, time, power and institutional access shape the opportunities from which agency is exercised. International well-being frameworks support keeping subjective experience alongside objective and relational conditions, but the reviewed set does not establish one causal structural model. [Sources: S2-A-SRC-007, S2-A-SRC-012, S2-A-SRC-013, S2-A-SRC-017; ledger: S2-CLM-006]

This creates a genuine philosophical tension. A subjective account respects people’s own appraisals but can accommodate adaptive expectations under deprivation. An objective list can reveal missing conditions but can become paternalistic, especially when designed far from the lives it judges.

Stage 2 adopts a limited common floor rather than a complete objective ideal. The floor comprises bodily integrity and basic health; protection from violence, humiliation and arbitrary domination; sufficient material security; recognition as a person of equal standing; access to relationships and assistance; and real opportunities to learn, act, participate and revise one’s direction. The precise institutional guarantees belong to later work.

Above that floor, the model preserves wide pluralism. It does not prescribe extroversion, wealth, conventional family life, professional ambition, religious belief, secular individualism or one balance between independence and obligation.

**Normative Judgement S2-NRM-001:** subjective experience has authority but not exclusive authority; a limited rights-and-capabilities floor may challenge reported satisfaction where credible options, safety or equal standing are absent.

## 7. Adolescence: A Life Now and a Life Becoming

Young people are not merely future adults. Present joy, safety, friendship, privacy, voice and relief from humiliation matter now. At the same time, adolescence is a period in which options, commitments, identities and control capacities continue to develop. Flourishing therefore includes both present quality of life and expanding future possibility.

Evidence associates family, peer, teacher and school relationships with adolescent outcomes and distinguishes these sources without establishing strict non-substitutability. Selection and influence are entangled, and relationships can be bidirectional: young people shape relationships as relationships shape them. **Empirical Finding `S2-CLM-007`, Moderate confidence; Western-heavy and school-enrolled scope.** [Sources: S2-B-SRC-004, S2-A-SRC-010, S2-B-SRC-011–S2-B-SRC-013, S2-B-SRC-018, S2-B-SRC-020; `S2-EVD-003`]

As an explicit Normative Judgement, Stage 2 rejects both **presentism**—treating any immediate preference or comfort as decisive—and **deferred life**—sacrificing the present person entirely to a predicted future self. The project adopts evolving agency: protection should be proportionate, explainable and open to review, while challenge should expand rather than gratuitously narrow future options. This settlement is not presented as an effect established by the relationship studies.

No maturity score follows. Age, puberty, confidence, compliance or one decision cannot establish an individual’s responsibility or capacity. Disabled, neurodivergent, LGBTQ+, migrant, racialised, economically marginalised, absent and disconnected young people remain underrepresented in much of the evidence. Their absence cannot be converted into sameness, deficit or a later implementation footnote.

## 8. Project Flourishing Model

The model has seven interdependent dimensions. They are a decision framework, not natural modules and not a measurement scale.

| Dimension | What the project values | What it refuses to substitute |
|---|---|---|
| Bodily and material security | health, rest, safety, sufficient resources, freedom from humiliation and violence | toughness, endurance or resilience rhetoric |
| Credible agency | voice, intelligible options, effective means, endorsement, revision and refusal | offered choice, confidence, persistence or compliance |
| Developing competence | growing understanding and effective, responsible action | grades, productivity, rank or self-belief alone |
| Recognition and care | safe support, reciprocal regard, privacy and non-dominating connection | popularity, contact volume, institutional loyalty or conformity |
| Meaning and orientation | coherence, direction, mattering and space to question commitments | one universal purpose, optimism or ideological certainty |
| Responsible contribution | age-appropriate participation, care, repair and concern for consequences | compulsory service, sacrificial identity or usefulness as worth |
| Plural identity and possibility | room to form, revise and inhabit diverse good lives with equal standing | a single cultural image of success |

These dimensions interact. Security can enable agency; agency can seek relationship; relationship can build competence; competence can enlarge contribution; meaning can organise all of them. They can also conflict. A coherent model must therefore include trade-off rules.

## 9. Known Value Trade-offs

| Tension | Stage 2 settlement |
|---|---|
| Satisfaction vs objective conditions | Take first-person appraisal seriously, but retain a limited floor of rights, security and real opportunity. |
| Agency vs care/protection | Use evolving agency, proportionate protection, explanation and review; never infer endorsement from compliance. |
| Autonomy vs obligation | Permit endorsed interdependence and durable duties; audit power, burden and credible refusal. |
| Competence vs well-being | Permit worthwhile challenge; reject avoidable harm and achievement as the measure of worth. |
| Belonging vs dissent | Value recognition and care while protecting privacy, difference, critique and exit. |
| Meaning vs truth/justice | Treat purpose as psychologically consequential but morally contestable. |
| Contribution vs exploitation | Value participation and repair only with fair burdens, voice and developmental proportionality. |
| Present welfare vs future options | Protect life now while expanding, not pre-emptively closing, future possibilities. |
| Common floor vs cultural pluralism | Specify thin protections and broad freedoms; review translation and local meaning claim by claim. |
| Measurement vs personhood | Use indicators descriptively for population monitoring or design review, never as a total learner score, diagnosis or optimisation target. |

## 10. Normative Commitments Register

1. **Equal standing:** every person has worth not conditional on achievement, conformity or usefulness. [`S2-NRM-008`]
2. **Floor and freedom:** secure minimum conditions while leaving substantial room for plural good lives.
3. **Evolving agency:** respect present voice and developing capacity, with proportionate and reviewable protection.
4. **Non-domination:** examine power, hidden burdens, humiliation and the credibility of dissent or exit.
5. **Responsible plurality:** freedom is constrained by the equal standing, safety and agency of others.
6. **Anti-reduction:** no metric or dimension may become a complete judgement of a person.

These are **Normative Judgements**. Evidence makes them informed, not inevitable.

## 11. What the Evidence Does Not Establish

- a universal, exhaustive list or fixed weighting of flourishing dimensions;
- that positive affect, life satisfaction, achievement, belonging, purpose or contribution is sufficient;
- that autonomy means independence or that choice proves endorsement;
- that competence equals confidence, productivity, credentials or rank;
- that cohesive families, communities, peer groups or institutions are caring or just;
- that hardship is needed for growth or that resilience excuses preventable adversity;
- that meaningful or prosocial action is voluntary, beneficial or morally worthy;
- that Western measures transport without cultural and linguistic testing;
- a causal diagnosis of an individual from any flourishing indicator;
- the purpose, content or architecture of education.

## 12. Uncertainties and Revision Triggers

The model should reopen if stronger evidence shows that a proposed dimension lacks transportability; if affected communities identify coercive or exclusionary use; if the common floor proves too thick for pluralism or too thin for protection; if downstream work converts dimensions into a composite score; or if new evidence materially changes adolescent, disability, cultural or structural interpretations.

Major open questions include how endorsement can be assessed under dependency; how relational and collective forms of agency should be represented; how care burdens and invisible contribution should be valued; how to protect chosen solitude; and how immediate welfare should trade against future option value without adult prediction swallowing adolescent voice.

## Conclusion

The most defensible story is not that flourishing has seven ingredients. It is that human lives go well when people can increasingly participate in authoring them—without having to author them alone, without being abandoned to whatever options power has left available, and without earning their worth through performance or service.

Flourishing is therefore neither private happiness nor social usefulness. It is a relationship between a person and a world: a body secure enough to act; capacities that can grow; relationships that recognise without engulfing; purposes that can survive questioning; responsibilities that take others seriously; and institutions that leave credible room to speak, refuse, repair and begin again.
