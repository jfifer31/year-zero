## 5. Normative questions, not authorised empirical conclusions

Stage 1 does not define responsibility, accountability, dignity or flourishing. It establishes only that:

- compliance does not disclose motive or moral endorsement;
- moral development is multi-process and culturally situated;
- contextual explanation does not by itself settle accountability.

Stage 2 must make its value judgements explicit, state alternatives and trade-offs, and avoid presenting them as Stage 1 findings. [Ledger: `S1-CLM-001`, `S1-CLM-016`, `S1-EVD-012`]

## 7. Claims not to overextend

Later stages must not:

- treat association as causation;
- infer ability, motivation, maturity, responsibility or potential from one performance, age, pubertal status, brain measure, biomarker, adversity history, diagnosis or demographic category;
- describe adolescents as uniformly irrational, reckless or peer-controlled;
- treat autonomy as unrestricted choice or absence of structure;
- describe rewards as uniformly harmful or uniformly effective;
- use retention as a complete proxy for understanding, judgement or far transfer;
- claim that sleep, stress reduction, movement or belonging independently guarantees attainment;
- treat belonging as mere proximity, popularity or a substitute for safeguarding or clinical support;
- treat physical placement as meaningful inclusion;
- universalise Western-developed motivation, identity, morality or belonging constructs;
- erase impairment and support needs in a difference-only account, or erase agency and person–environment relations in a deficit-only account;
- derive a subject, capability, pedagogy, assessment, policy or curriculum architecture directly from Stage 1.

## 8. Required cautions for Stage 2

1. Separate empirical evidence about human development and well-being from normative judgements about the good life.
2. Preserve both agency and context; neither moralising individualism nor context determinism is authorised.
3. Treat group evidence as probabilistic and transportability as claim-specific.
4. Keep missing populations visible, especially absent and disconnected learners, alternative provision, high-support-needs learners, non-English-language contexts and gender-diverse learners.
5. Treat all Stage 1 implications as constraints on reasoning, not ready-made design responses.

## 9. Rejected or non-authorised items

- A total or universal theory that all human performance is “interaction-produced.”
- A universal standard learner, motivational taxonomy, adolescent brain model, maturity threshold, identity sequence or moral framework.
- Responsibility or accountability as settled empirical findings.
- The complete tension table as one omnibus pass-forward claim.
- Any claim of educational effectiveness or curriculum design.

## 10. Revision triggers

Reopen Stage 1 if:

- evidence shows KS3–KS4 requires materially different developmental framing;
- formal review finds a passed constraint lacks cross-source convergence;
- a strong omitted review or replicated contradiction materially changes a central claim;
- new causal, longitudinal, cross-cultural or intersectional evidence materially narrows `S1-UNC-001`–`S1-UNC-008`;
- later implementation evidence tests and contradicts a contextual constraint;
- audit shows inadequate mitigation of `S1-RSK-001`–`S1-RSK-005`.
