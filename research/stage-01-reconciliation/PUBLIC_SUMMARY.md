# Stage 1R Public Summary — A Two-Layer Human Baseline

> **Status:** Passed with cautions. Stage 2 is authorised from this reconciliation only.

## What question did this stage ask?

Stage 1R compared two completed studies. Stage 1B looked broadly at recurring human action and social organisation outside education. Stage 1A looked at how human functioning appears in adolescent and educational settings.

The task was not to choose one study over the other. It was to identify what can be cautiously combined, what must remain specific to one context and what is still unresolved.

## What did the reconciliation find?

The draft uses two analytical layers. They are a provisional way to organise the evidence, not a discovered architecture of human nature.

**Layer one: candidate general patterns.** In the reviewed, culturally uneven evidence, human action appears embodied and cognitively bounded. The studies distinguish beliefs, opportunities, habits and relationships, and describe affiliation and boundaries, multiple routes to status and power, conditional cooperation and enforcement, and meaning-related commitments. The recurrence, form and interpretation of these patterns may differ across cultures, languages, ecologies, institutions, genders, classes and measures. None is established here as universal, innate, exhaustive or morally desirable.

**Layer two: adolescent and educational expression in the reviewed evidence.** Prior knowledge changes how demanding a task is. Remembering is not the same as transferring knowledge. Different motives can produce similar visible effort. Sleep, stress and emotion can affect observed functioning. The evidence rejects both the assumption that adolescents lack meaningful control and the assumption that control is deployed uniformly across people and situations. No single performance, score or category provides a complete account of an individual learner.

## Where are the studies compatible, and where did they converge?

The components are thematically compatible on several cautions, but not every item below is an independently convergent finding:

- behaviour does not transparently reveal one inner trait;
- cognition and action are bounded and condition-sensitive;
- agency is not simply willpower or unrestricted choice;
- the direction from affiliation toward care, indifference, exclusion or hostility remains unresolved;
- Stage 1B documents several status mechanisms, while Stage 1A separately distinguishes peer-status constructs;
- measurement influences what differences become visible.

This apparent convergence is not fully independent. At least five DOI-identical source records occur in both ledgers, some topics use overlapping concepts, and overlap among primary samples inside reviews was not assessed. The draft therefore does not treat repeated language as double evidence.

## What remains uncertain?

Major uncertainties include cultural transportability, the origins of recurrent patterns, how agency mechanisms interact, when affiliation becomes exclusion, how status converts into power, including gendered, domestic, care-based and less publicly visible power, when cooperation becomes extraction, how meaning differs from coercion, and how disability, language, gender, poverty and access interact.

## What should readers not conclude?

This work does not show that hierarchy, tribalism, punishment or any social institution is innate, inevitable or good. It does not define responsibility, justice, flourishing or education's purpose. It does not authorise subjects, capabilities, pedagogy, assessment or curriculum.

## What happens next?

Independent evidence, bias and adversarial reviewers required substantial corrections and then passed the revised package. Its pass-forward is now the sole Stage 1 authority for Stage 2.

## Plain-language takeaway

The draft offers a cautious, population-bounded starting point: the reviewed evidence supports examining embodiment, bounded cognition, relationships, social organisation and variation, while treating the two-layer frame and its cultural transportability as provisional. It does not establish a universal account of humans, adolescents or learners, and it does not answer what a good human life or a good education should be.
