## 2. Objective

Reconcile the reviewed Stage 1A education-facing human baseline and Stage 1B general human baseline into a traceable two-layer draft without collecting evidence, strengthening component claims, defining flourishing or deriving education's purpose.

## 3. Authorised inputs

| Input | Permitted use | Restriction |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1A six canonical files | Reviewed component pass-forward claims, assumptions, uncertainties, risks and cited ledger records | Component authority only; no independent Stage 2 authority |
| Stage 1B six canonical files | Reviewed component pass-forward claims, assumptions, uncertainties, risks and cited ledger records | Component authority only; no independent Stage 2 authority |
| Stage 1R guide and operating files | Method, classification and gate discipline | Not evidence |

No external source discovery, browsing, new evidence extraction or reinterpretation of uncited component material is authorised.

## 4. Questions

1. Which component claims describe a general human baseline?
2. Which are adolescent or education-context modifiers?
3. Where is convergence independent, partly dependent or merely terminological?
4. Which claims remain component-specific, contradictory, unresolved or excluded?
5. What minimum two-layer baseline can be proposed without beginning Stage 2?

## 5. Method

- Controlled claim comparison using component pass-forward statements and linked ledger records.
- Source-identity comparison by title and citation/DOI.
- Construct-overlap audit for autonomy, agency, belonging, status, culture and measurement.
- Conservative synthesis: merged confidence does not exceed component support and defaults to Moderate where scope differs.
- Explicit classification into the six Stage 1R categories.
- No source-count-based confidence inflation.
