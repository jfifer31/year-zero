| Permitted interpretation | Prohibited overextension |
|---|---|

## Explicit Stage 2 cautions

If this draft is formally authorised, Stage 2 must:

1. distinguish empirical evidence about human functioning from normative judgements about flourishing;
2. treat the two-layer baseline as constraints and questions, not a definition of the good life;
3. preserve culturally plural forms of autonomy, obligation, meaning and contribution;
4. keep the corpus's missing populations visible, including absent or disconnected learners, alternative provision, learners with high support needs, non-English-language contexts and gender-diverse learners; absence of evidence about these groups is not evidence of sameness or difference;
5. treat disability, impairment, neurotype, language, poverty and institutional access as potentially interacting with opportunity, measurement and observed functioning; do not reduce disability to difference alone, deficit alone, or a later implementation issue;
6. retain both agency and context without deterministic inference in either direction;
7. examine power and coercion without assuming every asymmetry is illegitimate;
8. avoid deriving education's purpose, capabilities or curriculum directly from recurrent human patterns;
9. retain every uncertainty and prohibited overextension above.

## Excluded items

- Responsibility, justice, dignity or flourishing as empirical conclusions.
- Any direct curriculum, capability, subject, pedagogy, assessment or policy conclusion.
- Any confidence increase based only on shared terminology or duplicated sources.
