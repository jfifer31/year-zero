## Executive Summary

Stage 1A and Stage 1B answer different questions. Stage 1B examines recurring patterns in human action and social organisation outside education. Stage 1A examines how cognition, motivation, embodiment, social life, development and variation become visible in adolescent and educational settings. Reconciliation therefore produces a two-layer baseline rather than averaging the components together.

The first layer is general and provisional. Across the culturally uneven reviewed evidence, human action is embodied and bounded; distinguishable control processes and available opportunities matter; sampled social networks include overlapping relations and boundaries; Stage 1B documents multiple routes to influence and conditional cooperation; and meaning-related practices have conditional associations with commitment. The directions from affiliation toward care or exclusion, and from meaning toward endorsement or coercion, remain unresolved. These are moderate-confidence, population-bounded findings and thematic compatibilities, not an exhaustive drive inventory or account of innateness.

The second layer concerns adolescent and educational expression. Prior knowledge changes effective processing demands; retention and transfer are distinct; motivation has different qualities; bodily state can alter observed functioning; adolescent control is meaningful but variably deployed; peers can amplify local norms; and no score, diagnosis, demographic category or isolated performance is a complete account of a learner. These are modifiers of how general human constraints appear in KS3–KS4 contexts, not exceptions to the general layer.

The components are thematically compatible on several cautions: observed behaviour does not transparently reveal one internal trait; cognition and control are bounded and condition-sensitive; agency is not reducible to will or choice; and measurement helps determine what becomes visible. Affiliation's direction toward care, indifference, exclusion or hostility remains unresolved, while status evidence is component-specific rather than independently convergent. At least five DOI-identical records recur across the ledgers, several constructs overlap, and underlying study or sample overlap inside reviews was not assessed. Compatibility is therefore not represented as independent convergence and never raises confidence by itself.

Stage 1R does not define flourishing, responsibility, justice or education's purpose. It carries those as explicit questions and cautions for Stage 2. The revised handoff passed evidence, classification, cultural-generalisability and adversarial review with cautions.

## 1. Reconciliation Question and Method

### Primary question

What minimum, two-layer human baseline survives comparison between the reviewed general-human and education-facing component studies without strengthening either component's claims?

### Method

The reconciliation compared only:

- Stage 1A `PASS_FORWARD.md` findings `PF-01`–`PF-07`, assumptions, uncertainties and prohibited overextensions;
- Stage 1B `PASS_FORWARD.md` findings `1B-PF-01`–`1B-PF-13`, working assumptions, normative protocols, uncertainties and prohibited overextensions;
- the component ledger records cited by those pass-forward statements.

Each merged candidate was classified as general human baseline, adolescent or education-context modifier, convergent, component-specific, contradictory or unresolved, or excluded. Convergence was audited at three levels: source identity, construct overlap and inferential independence. No new evidence was sought, and no component claim was strengthened.

### Method limits

- Stage 1A and Stage 1B used different questions, workstreams and inclusion strategies; absence from one component is not counterevidence.
- Shared terminology does not demonstrate the same mechanism.
- Source independence is assessed from ledger metadata and claim links, not from a new review of study samples.
- Stage 1B remains English-language and Western-heavy; Stage 1A also contains school-enrolled, Western and self-report concentrations.
- Reconciliation cannot repair evidence gaps. Gaps remain uncertainties or trigger return to a component stage.

## 2. Central Synthesis: Human Patterns, Situated Expression

The minimum defensible synthesis is that people act through bounded, embodied and relational systems whose observable forms depend on accumulated knowledge, available means, bodily state, social relations, institutions, development and measurement. This is an interpretive integration of reviewed findings, not a species-wide causal architecture.

The general layer prevents education from treating the learner as an isolated information processor. Humans regulate bodily demands; direct action with limited attention and control; form relations and boundaries; negotiate multiple kinds of rank; coordinate through expectations and institutions; and organise commitments through meanings that can be endorsed, imposed or contested. [Component records: `S1B-CLM-002`–`S1B-CLM-012`, `S1B-CLM-016`, `S1B-CLM-017`]

The modifier layer prevents the general account from becoming abstract. In educational settings, knowledge alters task burden, motivation alters what effort means, bodily state can alter deployment, and adolescence makes social evaluation and goal priorities especially relevant without eliminating reason. Measurement, opportunity and institutional access affect what becomes visible, while population patterns remain incomplete guides to individuals. [Component records: `S1-CLM-001`–`S1-CLM-015`; authorised component interface: `PF-01`–`PF-07`]

This synthesis rejects three reductions:

1. **Trait reduction:** behaviour or performance is not a complete reading of ability, motive, maturity or character.
2. **Context reduction:** context does not erase agency, knowledge differences, impairment or responsibility.
3. **nature-by-recurrence:** recurrence does not establish innateness, adaptation, inevitability, legitimacy or value.

**Claim type:** Working Assumption for reconciliation. **Confidence:** Moderate. **Revision trigger:** formal review finds that this integration creates false convergence, suppresses a material contradiction or implies a causal system not present in the component records. [Ledger: `S1R-ASM-001`]

## 4. Source-Independence Audit

The two ledgers contain 120 Stage 1A sources and 185 Stage 1B sources. Metadata comparison identified at least five DOI-identical records:

- `S1-SRC-009` / `S1B-W2-SRC-006` — extrinsic rewards and intrinsic motivation;
- `S1-SRC-103` / `S1B-W8-SRC-024` — cross-cultural developmental psychology;
- `S1-SRC-100` / `S1B-W8-SRC-018` — ontogeny of prosocial behaviour across diverse societies;
- `S1-SRC-101` / `S1B-W8-SRC-002` — WEIRD sampling critique.
- `S1-SRC-102` / `S1B-W8-SRC-004` — cross-cultural developmental-methods call to action.

All five pairs share exact DOI identifiers. Direct duplication was checked by title and DOI. Overlap among underlying studies or samples inside reviews was not assessed from ledger metadata; inferential independence therefore remains unknown beyond the identified direct duplicates. Shared terminology is also insufficient: autonomy, belonging, agency, culture and status are operationalised differently across the components.

Independence judgements are therefore:

- **Direct duplication not identified, but independence unknown:** embodied regulation, bounded cognition, affiliation and rank; underlying study/sample overlap was not assessed.
- **Partly dependent:** agency/motivation and measurement/variation because of both shared sources and repeated constructs.
- **Not convergence-tested:** cooperation/enforcement, meaning/ritual, adolescent development and educational transfer because one component supplies the substantive claim.

The result is deliberately conservative: thematic compatibility can increase coherence, but it does not raise confidence unless the underlying sources, samples and constructs are positively shown to be independent.

## 5. Two-Layer Human Baseline

### Layer 1 — General human drives and social structures

1. **Embodied regulation.** Reviewed evidence includes anticipatory and reactive regulatory patterns. Defensive response, conscious fear, wanting, liking and reward learning are partly separable. Physiological signals do not transparently disclose experience, motive or prognosis. **Classification:** partial thematic convergence; mechanisms and populations differ. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-002`, `S1B-CLM-003`; `S1-CLM-010`]

2. **Bounded action and cognition.** Attention, information and computational capacity are limited. Action commonly involves distinguishable control beliefs, available means, opportunities, habits and cue-sensitive planning. No single agency trait or universal strategy inventory is authorised. **Classification:** general-human component finding plus education-context modifier; not convergence-tested at mechanism level. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-004`, `S1B-CLM-005`; `S1-CLM-001`, `S1-CLM-004`]

3. **Affiliation and boundaries.** Across reviewed settings, social networks commonly include overlapping kin and non-kin relations. Categories can alter expectations without inherently causing hostility. The conditions under which affiliation is associated with care, indifference, exclusion or hostility remain unresolved. **Classification:** partial thematic convergence; mechanisms and populations differ. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-006`; `S1-CLM-011`–`S1-CLM-013`; `S1R-UNC-004`]

4. **Plural rank and influence.** Prestige, dominance, authority, inherited position and resource control are distinguishable, non-exhaustive routes to influence. Under some conditions advantage becomes self-reinforcing; resistance and levelling can constrain domination. **Classification:** Stage 1B component-specific general baseline with a separate Stage 1A peer-status modifier. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-007`, `S1B-CLM-008`; `S1-EVD-019`]

5. **Conditional collective action.** Cooperation and punishment vary with expectations, reciprocity, boundaries, rules, sanction structure, power and retaliation. **Classification:** component-specific general human baseline. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-009`, `S1B-CLM-010`]. The separate normative judgement `S1R-NRM-001` states that collective effect does not establish legitimacy or justice.

6. **Meaning and commitment without a universal taxonomy.** Predominantly Western research distinguishes coherence, direction or purpose, and mattering. Separate evidence links ritual, protected values and contribution conditionally with commitment-related outcomes; coercion and exclusion remain possible. **Classification:** component-specific and culturally bounded. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1B-CLM-011`, `S1B-CLM-016`; Stage 1A motivation caution through `PF-03` and `S1-CLM-001`–`S1-CLM-003`]

7. **Structured variation and measurement.** Measures and construct equivalence affect which differences are visible and comparable. Ecology and institutions are associated with observed variation, but causal partitioning remains unresolved. Population regularities do not constitute complete individual profiles. **Classification:** directly source-dependent convergence on a measurement caution; substantive transportability remains unresolved. **Confidence:** High for the narrow measurement caution; Moderate for transportability and causal interpretation. [`S1B-CLM-012`, `S1B-CLM-017`; `S1-CLM-014`, `S1-CLM-015`]

### Layer 2 — Adolescent and educational-expression modifiers

1. **Knowledge changes effective demand.** Prior knowledge can reduce effective processing burden; retention, understanding and transfer are distinct, and unfamiliar transfer is not automatic. **Confidence:** High within the reviewed educational and cognitive scope. [`S1-CLM-004`, `S1-CLM-005`]

2. **Motivational quality changes the meaning of effort.** Interest, endorsed value, competence beliefs, goals, pressure and incentives are distinguishable. Visible compliance does not disclose endorsement. **Confidence:** High for differentiation; Moderate for causal and cultural interpretation. [`S1-CLM-001`–`S1-CLM-003`]

3. **Observed functioning is state-sensitive.** Sleep, stress and emotion can alter functioning, but no isolated bodily state, adversity history, biomarker or performance diagnoses potential. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1-CLM-010`]

4. **Adolescent control is real and variably deployed.** Development, task, emotion and social context can affect reliability and priorities; age and puberty do not establish one maturity timetable. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1-CLM-006`–`S1-CLM-009`]

5. **Peers and educational institutions alter social stakes.** Peer contexts can amplify prosocial or risky norms. Mainly school-based belonging evidence concerns recognition, accepted identity and safety, while popularity, acceptance and friendship remain distinct. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1-CLM-009`, `S1-CLM-011`–`S1-CLM-013`]

6. **Educational observation is an inference problem.** An isolated performance can reflect knowledge, processing demand, motive, bodily state, development, opportunity and context, but the evidence cannot diagnose their contribution in an individual case. **Confidence:** Moderate. [`S1-CLM-004`, `S1-CLM-015`, `S1-ASM-002`]

7. **There is no complete standard-learner profile.** Diagnostic and demographic categories and population patterns can be useful but do not determine an individual's abilities, motives, interests or needs. **Confidence:** High for the non-deterministic caution. [`S1-CLM-014`, `S1-CLM-015`]

## 6. Contradictions, Unresolved Tensions and Exclusions

### Contradictory or unresolved

- **Agency:** The components support distinguishable control and motivational constructs, but not one integrated causal model of agency. Western assumptions about authorship and independence remain a material boundary.
- **Meaning and responsibility:** Stage 1B describes meaning-related constructs and commitment practices; Stage 1A shows that compliance does not disclose endorsement. Neither defines responsibility, accountability or a good life.
- **Status and belonging:** Recognition can support affiliation while status systems can reproduce exclusion and power. The evidence does not establish when recognition becomes domination or when group attachment becomes hostility.
- **Context and causation:** Both components show condition-sensitive patterns, but neither can partition the effects of person, culture, ecology, institution, selection, history and measurement in a general model.

### Excluded from downstream use

- the six-domain frame as an empirical architecture or exhaustive drive inventory;
- any claim that recurrence proves innateness, adaptation or evolutionary function;
- a universal hierarchy drive, natural group unit, copying module, meaning taxonomy or moral system;
- responsibility, justice, dignity or flourishing as settled empirical findings;
- claims that cooperation, punishment, ritual, contribution or hierarchy are necessarily beneficial or legitimate;
- a universal adolescent brain model, maturity threshold or standard learner;
- direct curriculum, capability, pedagogy, assessment or education-purpose conclusions.

## 7. Uncertainties Carried Forward

The reconciliation retains the component uncertainties rather than collapsing them. The principal combined uncertainties are:

1. cross-cultural transportability and construct equivalence (`S1-UNC-007`; `S1B-UNC-001`, `S1B-UNC-003`);
2. origin: preparedness, common demands, learning, diffusion, institutions or artefact (`S1B-UNC-002`);
3. causal pathways among autonomy, competence, prior success, relationship and opportunity (`S1-UNC-001`; `S1B-UNC-004`);
4. authentic transfer beyond bounded tasks (`S1-UNC-002`);
5. adolescent sensitive periods and maturity variation (`S1-UNC-003`);
6. embodied-state effect size, causality and developmental specificity (`S1-UNC-005`);
7. mechanisms linking belonging to outcomes and affiliation to care or hostility (`S1-UNC-006`; `S1B-UNC-005`);
8. status conversion, gendered/domestic power and less visible authority (`S1B-UNC-006`);
9. cooperation versus extraction under monitoring and sanctions (`S1B-UNC-007`);
10. endorsed meaning and contribution versus obligation or coercion (`S1B-UNC-008`);
11. intersectional variation in disability, neurotype, language, gender, poverty, culture and access (`S1-UNC-008`);
12. causal direction among identity, mental health and context (`S1-UNC-004`).

## 8. Prohibited Overextensions

Later work must not:

- infer a fixed trait, motive, maturity, potential or subjective state from one behaviour, task, score, diagnosis, biomarker, age or demographic category;
- treat contextual explanation as proof that agency, responsibility, impairment or knowledge differences are absent;
- treat recurrence as universality, innateness, adaptation, inevitability, legitimacy or value;
- treat affiliation as inevitable tribalism or belonging as mere proximity, popularity or a substitute for safeguarding;
- collapse prestige, dominance, authority, inheritance and resources into one hierarchy mechanism;
- infer justice from cooperation, contribution, punishment, ritual or stability;
- universalise Western autonomy, meaning, identity, morality or measurement constructs;
- treat retention as understanding or transfer;
- derive education's purpose, flourishing, capabilities, subjects, pedagogy, assessment or curriculum from this baseline.

## 9. Explicit Stage 2 Cautions

If formal review later authorises this reconciliation, Stage 2 must:

1. use the baseline as constraints and questions, not as a ready-made definition of flourishing;
2. separate empirical findings from normative judgements about worth, responsibility, justice and the good life;
3. preserve plural reasonable accounts of autonomy, obligation, meaning and social contribution;
4. examine who benefits, who bears costs and whether refusal is credible without assuming every asymmetry is illegitimate;
5. avoid turning recurrent tendencies into educational aims without additional normative reasoning;
6. retain all cultural, causal, developmental, measurement and intersectional uncertainties;
7. avoid interpreting adolescent or educational modifiers as universal properties of all humans.

## Conclusion

Stage 1A and Stage 1B do not combine into a catalogue of innate drives or a recipe for education. Together they support a more modest two-layer baseline. Human action is bounded, embodied, relational and socially organised, while the forms and consequences of regulation, agency, affiliation, rank, cooperation and commitment vary across settings and measures. Adolescence and education add specific conditions under which those general patterns become visible: knowledge changes effective demand, motives change the meaning of effort, bodily state changes deployment, social audiences change stakes and one performance remains an incomplete account of the learner.

This is enough to discipline Stage 2, but not to answer it. Flourishing, responsibility, justice and education's purpose remain normative work. The revised reconciliation package passed formal review with cautions and is the sole Stage 1 authority for Stage 2.
