## 2. Purpose of this pass-forward

This memo passes forward the reviewed Stage 4 synthesis: how human life may be experienced and organised under plausible AI-shaped future conditions. Stage 5 may use these findings only to test possible human invariants across futures.

Stage 4 does not authorise curriculum design, capability derivation, subjects, pedagogy, assessment, policy prescription, AI-use guidance for learners or a single predicted future.

## 6. Claims not to overextend

| Claim | Permitted interpretation | Prohibited overextension |
|---|---|---|
| AI mediates action. | AI systems may increasingly sit between human intention and social/institutional action. | AI inevitably removes agency or automatically expands it. |
| Verification becomes more important. | Some domains will require more checking of outputs, identities, claims and processes. | Everything becomes unknowable or truth collapses. |
| Output becomes less readable. | Visible products may reveal less about unaided competence where AI assistance is common. | Assessment is impossible or traditional knowledge no longer matters. |
| Status shifts. | Status competition may reorganise around new scarce goods. | One new AI hierarchy is inevitable or natural. |
| Belonging remains unresolved. | More interaction does not guarantee recognition or care. | AI companions are necessarily harmful or necessarily adequate substitutes. |
| Access shapes futures. | Infrastructure and institutional conditions strongly mediate lived AI experience. | One universal AI future applies to all learners or families. |
| Adolescence modifies experience. | Young people face specific pressures around identity, agency, status and institutional dependency. | Adolescents are uniformly vulnerable, irrational, incapable or peer-controlled. |

## 7. Required cautions for Stage 5

- Do not treat recurring Stage 4 pressures as invariants until Stage 5 tests them.
- Preserve the two-layer structure: general human pressures and adolescent/educational modifiers.
- Keep Stage 2’s anti-scoring and anti-productivity commitments visible.
- Keep Stage 3’s capability-versus-adoption distinction visible.
- Treat access, verification and institutional permission as first-order conditions.
- Do not derive capabilities, curriculum purposes, subjects, pedagogy or assessment.
- Do not turn condition models into a preferred future.
