**Forecast-anchor notes for scenario years:**

- **2029** is used as an early-transition year because late-2020s forecasts and stress tests make it a useful near-term setting for modelling AI as socially disruptive infrastructure while institutions still look familiar. Anchors include Ray Kurzweil’s continued 2029 human-level AI forecast, the AI 2027 scenario exercise, METR long-task-horizon work and Grace et al.’s survey milestones.
- **2037** is used as a system-reorganisation year because it sits after the plausible late-2020s capability shock and aligns with Grace et al.’s 10 percent occupational-automation stress-test date plus frontier-leader claims that powerful AI may create major 2030s institutional pressure.
- **2045** is used as a mature-divergence year because it is the well-known Kurzweil singularity horizon and sits close to Grace et al.’s 2047 median date for unaided machines outperforming humans at every task. It is also far enough out to model radical infrastructure such as orbital compute while preserving major engineering and governance caveats.

**Forecast-anchor sources used for dating only:**

- Ray Kurzweil, `The Singularity Is Nearer`, and public discussion of his 2029 and 2045 forecasts.
- Sam Altman, `The Intelligence Age`, 2024.
- Dario Amodei, `Machines of Loving Grace`, 2024.
- `AI 2027` scenario project.
- METR, `Measuring AI Ability to Complete Long Tasks`, 2025.
- Grace et al., `Thousands of AI Authors on the Future of AI`, 2024.
- Space or orbital data-centre feasibility sources used only as 2045 radical-infrastructure scenario variables with strong caveats.
